Postdoc (Marie Sklodowska-Curie Fellow) | University of Amsterdam

I am a Marie Sklodowska-Curie fellow (a postdoc) in Max Welling's group (AMLab) at University of Amsterdam.

I am interested in machine learning, with special focus on deep learning, Bayesian inference and image analysis. Check my scientific profile.

My research is financed by the European Union within the Marie Sklodowska-Curie Action (Individual Fellowship), the project entitled "Deep Learning and Bayesian Inference for Medical Imaging" (DeeBMED).

Scientific profile

I have obtained Ph.D. ("Incremental Knowledge Extraction from Data for Non-Stationary Objects") from Wroclaw University of Technology in 2013 (with honours). After Ph.D. studies I worked on ensemble classifiers, probabilistic modelling and deep learning (with special interest in Boltzmann machines) and its application to credit scoring, medicine (clinical data) and image analysis. Now I focus on deep learning and Bayesian inference for medical imaging (see DeeBMED).

Deep Learning and Bayesian Inference for Medical Imaging (DeeBMED)

The aim of the project is to apply current state-of-the-art techniques of deep learning to medical imaging. Additionally, we aim at enriching deep learning models with Bayesian inference to better reflect uncertainty in medical data.
